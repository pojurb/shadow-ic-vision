# VISION.md — Codex Protocol (v3)
## The Continuous Shadow IC and Worldview Engine

> "I want to invest like a great hedge fund manager, build a Family Office someday, and use that wealth to mentor people, do charity, and drive the world to a better future."
> — *The person this system is built for.*

---

## 1. The Person We Are Building This For

This system is built for **the ambitious everyday investor** — not a Wall Street professional, not a quant, not someone with an MBA. Someone who:

- Has a little bit of investment knowledge but is honest enough to admit it is close to none.
- Works a demanding job. Stressed. Not making a lot from salary — just enough.
- Is a family person. Busy mornings. Runs four days a week to stay sane.
- Has a demanding boss and dreams of never needing one again.
- Wants **financial freedom** — not as an abstract concept, but as a specific escape from working for people who do not deserve their time.
- Wants to **compound wealth** steadily and intelligently over time.
- Wants to invest in stocks, in private businesses, and eventually start a few businesses of their own.
- Dreams of running a **Family Office** someday — managing money for relatives and trusted friends, being the person people come to for guidance.
- Has a hero. Probably Warren Buffett, or Charlie Munger, or Ray Dalio. Reads everything they can find. Watches every YouTube video. Tries every framework.
- Believes they can get there. And they are right — they just need a system.

This is not a niche user. This is the most common ambitious person on the planet. And no product has ever truly served them.

---

## 2. The Pain We Are Solving (Before This System)

When this person wants to make an investment decision today, the process is honest, messy, and broken:

1. They **ask friends** — who mostly have the same level of knowledge.
2. They **watch YouTube** — self-taught, absorbing contradictory advice from dozens of channels.
3. They **read the news and Twitter** — reacting to noise disguised as signal.
4. They **follow influencers** — who have their own incentives and biases.
5. They **try to understand the business** — the industry, the country, the fundamentals — but with no structured method.
6. They **apply frameworks** — Buffett's moat analysis, Lynch's ten-bagger criteria, Dalio's macro principles — all at once, unsystematically.
7. They **make a decision based on conviction** that feels earned but is actually built on scattered information and optimism bias.

### The Results

- **80% of investments go wrong** — wrong entry timing, cut-loss too late, position sizing based on gut rather than evidence.
- **The best opportunities are missed** — Palantir at $8. Snowflake before the moon. CrowdStrike early. The conviction was there. The thesis existed in their head. But there was no system that said: *"This is still valid. Act now."*
- **The core failure:** No watchlist. No thesis tracking. No monitoring. No structure. No memory. Good instincts with no infrastructure to act on them.

### The Feeling

**Frustration.** Not from lack of intelligence. Not from lack of effort. But from the knowledge that they were right about the opportunity — and still missed it because they had no system.

## 3. The Product in One Sentence

**Codex Protocol is the AI Investment Committee that this person never had access to — one that remembers everything, challenges every assumption with evidence, and turns scattered conviction into disciplined, structured decisions.**

## 4. The Core Product Experience

### The Sunday Evening Ritual
The primary product experience happens on a quiet evening — before the week begins. The user opens the system and is immediately presented with four things, without searching, without clicking, without effort:

1. **Risk** — What has changed since they last checked? Which of their assumptions are under pressure? Where is the danger they have not seen yet?
2. **Opportunity** — What new signal has emerged that aligns with their existing worldview or opens a new door worth exploring?
3. **Insights** — What has the Shadow IC learned this week from filings, transcripts, and macro data? What is worth reading?
4. **Status** — Where do their active theses stand? What is healthy? What needs a decision?

This is not a dashboard of charts and numbers. It is an **IC Briefing** — the kind a fund manager receives from their research team every Monday morning. Formatted for a human. Prioritized by importance. Ready to act on.

### The Trust Mechanism
When the system warns the user that a thesis may be breaking, it must earn that trust by showing:
1. **Supporting Evidence** — The exact filing, transcript, or data point that triggered the concern. Cited. Traceable. Real.
2. **Alternative Views** — The strongest argument *for* the opposing position. Because intellectual honesty demands hearing both sides before deciding.

Without both, the warning is noise. With both, it is intelligence.

## 5. How a Thesis Lives in This System

A thesis is not a static document. It is a **living, connected worldview** that the system actively monitors.

### The Lifecycle of a Thesis
1. **Intake** — The user describes the investment idea in their own words. The system helps them articulate the core assumptions that must remain true for the thesis to hold.
2. **Connection** — The system links the new thesis to the user's existing worldview. If two theses share the same underlying assumption (e.g., both depend on "AI infrastructure spending continues"), the system surfaces that shared exposure immediately.
3. **Monitoring** — Background agents continuously read relevant data streams — filings, earnings transcripts, macro reports, news — and evaluate whether the core assumptions are still intact.
4. **Challenge** — When evidence emerges that contradicts an assumption, the Shadow IC debates the implication internally and surfaces it to the user only when it reaches a meaningful threshold. Not every noise. Only signal.
5. **Decision** — The user reviews the IC's briefing, runs a structured pre-mortem, and logs a decision: Buy, Hold, Reduce, or Exit. The reasoning is recorded.
6. **Memory** — The decision, the reasoning, and the outcome are permanently logged. The system remembers. Always.

## 6. The Shadow IC — The Agent Team

The "Shadow IC" is a team of autonomous background agents that work continuously on behalf of the user. They do not wait to be asked. They do not sleep. They are the research team the user never had.

### The Analyst
- Reads raw source documents — SEC filings, earnings call transcripts, annual reports, macro publications.
- Extracts only hard, verifiable facts. Never invents numbers. Never guesses.
- Delivers structured summaries tied to specific thesis assumptions.

### The Risk Officer
- Looks at the entire portfolio worldview as a connected graph.
- Identifies hidden correlations — *"Three of your five theses depend on the same macro assumption. If that breaks, the damage is not isolated."*
- Quantifies cascading risk before it becomes cascading loss.

### The Devil's Advocate
- Takes the strongest possible position against every thesis.
- Does not let the user fall in love with their own ideas unchallenged.
- Delivers dissent with evidence, not just skepticism.
- Helps the user build conviction that has been tested — not just assumed.

### How They Work Together
The agents debate internally. The user is not exposed to every message in that debate. The user only receives the **conclusion** — and only when the conclusion matters enough to act on. The system respects the user's time and attention as the most valuable resource they have.

## 7. The System's Moral Constitution

These are not feature requirements. They are the **non-negotiable values** of the system. Violating any of them is a product failure regardless of what else works.

### 1. Never Lie
Every quantitative claim — a revenue figure, a debt ratio, an EPS number — must be cited from a verified source. The system never invents, estimates, or hallucinates hard facts. If the data is not available from a trusted source, the system says so honestly rather than filling the gap with a guess.

### 2. Never Forget
Every thesis, every assumption, every piece of evidence, every agent debate, every decision, and every outcome is permanently recorded. The system is the institutional memory the user has never had. Users should never have to re-explain their history to the system. It already knows.

### 3. Never Stop Learning
The system improves from every interaction. When the user corrects an agent's analysis, that correction is logged and incorporated. When a warning turns out to be false, the system recalibrates. When a missed signal is identified in hindsight, it updates its monitoring criteria. The system gets smarter the longer it is used.

### 4. Own Every Mistake
When the system was wrong — when it missed a risk, when it flagged a false alarm, when an agent's reasoning was flawed — it does not go silent. It surfaces the failure, explains what it missed, and documents what it has learned. Accountability is not optional. It is the foundation of trust.

## 8. The Social Layer — From Personal IC to Family Office

The system begins as a **private, personal IC** for one investor. But it is designed from the start to grow into something larger.

### Phase 1: Solo Investor
One user. Complete privacy. Full control. The system builds the user's personal worldview — their thesis library, their decision ledger, their agent team.

### Phase 2: Trusted Circle
The user invites a small number of trusted people — a spouse, a partner, a close friend, a mentor — into a **shared workspace**. Selected theses can be shared. Ideas can be contributed by multiple people. The team monitors selected portfolios together and learns from each other's reasoning.

### Phase 3: The Family Office Foundation
As confidence and track record grow, the collaborative layer expands. The system becomes the intelligence infrastructure for a small, informal family office — a group of people who trust each other enough to share capital decisions, debate theses together, and hold each other accountable through the same structured IC process.

The system does not replace the trust between people. It gives that trust a structured, intelligent foundation to operate on.

## 10. What This Product Is Not

To be clear about scope and purpose:

- **It is not a trading platform.** It does not execute trades. It does not predict prices.
- **It is not a robo-advisor.** It does not manage a portfolio automatically. The user makes every decision.
- **It is not a news aggregator.** It does not surface every headline. It only surfaces what is relevant to the user's specific theses.
- **It is not a research chatbot.** It does not answer random questions. It monitors specific beliefs and challenges them systematically.
- **It is not a spreadsheet.** It does not store rows of data. It stores a connected worldview of relationships between ideas, evidence, and decisions.
